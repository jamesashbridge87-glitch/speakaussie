# Australian Job Interview Guide

## Before the Interview

### Research
- Company website, mission, values
- Recent news or achievements
- The role requirements
- Who you'll be meeting with
- Industry context

### Preparation
- Print copies of your resume
- Prepare 2-3 questions to ask
- Plan your route (arrive 5-10 min early)
- Dress code: smart casual to business (depends on industry)

## The Interview Process

### Greeting
- Firm handshake
- Eye contact and smile
- "G'day" or "Hi, nice to meet you" both work
- Use first names unless told otherwise

### Small Talk
Australians often start with casual chat:
- "Did you find the place okay?"
- "How's your day going?"
- Keep responses brief and positive
- This is part of assessing cultural fit

### Common Questions

**Tell me about yourself**
- 2-minute summary
- Focus on relevant experience
- End with why you're interested in this role

**Why do you want to work here?**
- Show you've researched the company
- Connect to your career goals
- Be genuine, not generic

**Behavioural Questions (STAR Method)**
- Situation: Set the scene
- Task: What was your responsibility
- Action: What you specifically did
- Result: The outcome (quantify if possible)

Examples:
- "Tell me about a time you dealt with a difficult customer"
- "Describe a situation where you had to meet a tight deadline"
- "Give an example of when you showed initiative"

**Strengths and Weaknesses**
- Strengths: Give examples to back them up
- Weaknesses: Show self-awareness and how you're improving

**Where do you see yourself in 5 years?**
- Show ambition but be realistic
- Connect to growth within the company
- Avoid sounding like you'll leave quickly

### Questions to Ask
Good questions show interest:
- "What does a typical day look like?"
- "What's the team culture like?"
- "What are the biggest challenges in this role?"
- "Is there room for growth and development?"
- "What do you enjoy about working here?"

Avoid asking about salary/benefits in first interview unless they bring it up.

## Australian Interview Style

### What They're Looking For
1. **Can you do the job?** (Skills and experience)
2. **Will you fit in?** (Cultural fit with team)
3. **Are you motivated?** (Genuine interest)
4. **Can you communicate?** (Clear and confident)

### Cultural Fit Matters
- Australians hire people they'd want to work with
- Being likeable matters
- Show you're a team player
- Demonstrate you can have a laugh

### The Right Balance
**Do:**
- Be confident but not arrogant
- Share achievements with context
- Use "we" as well as "I"
- Be specific with examples
- Show enthusiasm

**Don't:**
- Brag or oversell
- Be overly formal or stiff
- Badmouth previous employers
- Give vague or evasive answers
- Pretend to know things you don't

## Common Aussie Interview Phrases

### From the Interviewer
- "Tell us a bit about yourself" - Brief professional summary
- "What brings you to us?" - Why this company
- "How do you reckon you'd handle..." - Hypothetical scenario
- "What are you after in your next role?" - Career goals
- "Any questions for us?" - Your turn to ask

### Useful Responses
- "I reckon my experience in X would help because..."
- "To be honest, I haven't done exactly that, but..."
- "Fair question - let me think about that..."
- "That's a good point - in my last role we..."

### Ending the Interview
- "Thanks heaps for your time"
- "I really enjoyed learning more about the role"
- "When can I expect to hear back?"
- Firm handshake on the way out

## After the Interview

### Follow Up
- Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- Keep it brief: thank them, reiterate interest
- Reference something specific from the conversation

### If You Don't Hear Back
- Wait the timeframe they mentioned
- One polite follow-up is fine
- "Just following up on..." is acceptable

### If You Get the Job
- Negotiate professionally if needed
- "I was hoping for closer to X" is direct but acceptable
- Get the offer in writing before resigning elsewhere

### If You Don't Get It
- Ask for feedback (many will provide it)
- Thank them for the opportunity
- Keep the door open for future roles
