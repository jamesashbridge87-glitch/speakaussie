### 3.2. How to Prevent Churn

SpeakAussie can directly counter these churn drivers:

*   **To counter lack of speaking practice:** Develop robust scenarios that simulate real Australian workplace conversations.

*   **To counter billing issues:** Implement transparent, user-friendly billing with clear communication.
*   **To counter lack of progress:** Build in features that allow users to track their improvement and celebrate their wins.

## 4. User Behavior: The 15-Minute Sweet Spot

Understanding *how* and *when* users learn is critical for designing an engaging product.

*   **Optimal Session Length:** Research from Duolingo and user forums indicates that **15 minutes per day** is the ideal session length. It is substantial enough to feel productive but short enough to fit into a busy schedule. Sessions under 10 minutes feel ineffective, while those over 30 minutes lead to burnout.
*   **Learning Times:** Learning is spread throughout the day, indicating a need for flexibility.
    *   **Commute/Breaks:** 5-15 minute micro-sessions.
    *   **Evenings/Weekends:** 15-30+ minute deep-dive sessions.
*   **Format Preference:** Users want a mix of formats. The core experience should be structured lessons, supplemented by quick-practice games and immersive content. Forcing a single format creates friction.

**Product Implication:** Design a flexible experience. The core lesson path should be based on ~15 minute modules, but offer shorter, snackable exercises and longer, more immersive content to fit the user's available time and context.
