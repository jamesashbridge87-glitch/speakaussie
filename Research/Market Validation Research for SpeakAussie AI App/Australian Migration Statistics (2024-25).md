# Australian Migration Statistics (2024-25)

## Key Data Points from ABS

### Net Overseas Migration
- **2024-25**: 306,000 net gain (down from 429,000 in 2023-24)
- Represents 28% decline year-on-year
- Below financial year cap of 538,000 people

### Migrant Arrivals (2024-25)
- **Total arrivals**: 568,000 (down 14% from 661,000 in 2023-24)
- **Temporary visa holders**: 363,000 (down from 458,000)
- **Permanent visa holders**: 88,000 (down from 91,000)
- **Australian citizens**: 64,000 (up from 63,000)
- **NZ citizens**: 53,000 (up from 49,000)

### Temporary Visa Breakdown (2024-25)
- **International students**: 157,000 (largest group, down from 204,000 in 2023-24)
- **Working holiday makers**: 78,000
- **Temporary skilled**: 46,000
- **Visitors**: 56,000

### Historical Context (Pre-pandemic average)
- Average annual arrivals: 515,000
- Temporary visa arrivals: 307,000
- Permanent visa arrivals: 92,000
- Australian citizen arrivals: 77,000
- NZ citizen arrivals: 32,000

### Migrant Departures (2024-25)
- **Total departures**: 263,000 (up 13% from 232,000)
- **Temporary visa holders**: 148,000
- **Australian citizens**: 82,000
- **Permanent visa holders**: 19,000
- **NZ citizens**: 15,000

## Key Insight
The decline in arrivals is driven primarily by:
1. Reduction in international student intake (policy change)
2. Fewer temporary visa arrivals
3. Increase in departures (post-study work visa holders leaving after eligibility expires)

## Source
Australian Bureau of Statistics - Overseas Migration, 2024-25 financial year
Released: 19 December 2025
